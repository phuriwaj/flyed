## Day 1 — Arrival Bangkok

Arrive Suvarnabhumi Airport. Transfer to hotel near the Chao Phraya River in the Banglamphu district. Evening boat along the river at dusk — skyline views of Wat Arun's porcelain tiling and the Rama VIII Bridge cable stays. Welcome dinner at a riverside restaurant in the Phra Athit area — tom yum goong and green curry served while a traditional fon ensemble performs. The trip director introduces the cultural immersion framework for the week ahead.

## Day 2 — Grand Palace & Temple Heritage

Morning guided visit to the Grand Palace complex and Wat Phra Kaew (Temple of the Emerald Buddha) — students receive a guided worksheet on Chakri dynasty symbolism, Buddhist iconography, and the architectural fusion of Thai, European, and Chinese design elements visible throughout the complex. Afternoon visit to Wat Pho — students observe and sketch the 46m Reclining Buddha, then participate in a guided 15-minute meditation session in the temple grounds led by a resident monk (via interpreter). Evening market walk at Asiatique the Riverfront — students observe the differences between this purpose-built night market and Bangkok's older Chatuchak and Pratunam markets.

## Day 3 — Bangkok to Isan Plateau

Early morning flight to Khon Kaen (1 hour 10 minutes) on Thai AirAsia or Bangkok Airways. Transfer by air-conditioned coach to Ban Phai district (45 minutes). Afternoon orientation with local Isan host families — families arrive at the school meeting point with traditional welcome garlands (pha khao ma). Introduction to the Lao Phuan dialect — tonal differences from central Thai, basic greeting vocabulary. Students receive a simple conversation guide and are paired with their host sibling for the evening. Dinner with host families in their homes.

## Day 4 — Silk Weaving & Mekong Life

Morning visit to a silk-weaving cooperative in Sikhiu district — students observe the complete process: silk worm cultivation on mulberry leaves, cocoon sorting, ikat dyeing with natural dyes (madang bark, indigo), and weaving on traditional back-strap looms. Students try their hand at a simplified weaving pattern. Afternoon travel to the Mekong River at Ban Had Ngam — riverbank community walk with a local fisher who demonstrates throw-net casting and explains seasonal fishing patterns. Students record observations in their field journals. Evening homestay activities — students join their host family's evening routine (cooking, animal feeding, or local TV).

## Day 5 — Phimai Historical Park & Community Work

Morning visit to Phimai Historical Park — the 11th-century laterite Khmer temple complex at the heart of the ancient Angkorian road network. A local museum curator leads an archaeological workshop: students handle replica stone carvings, interpret inscription transcriptions, and debate whether Phimai should be considered a Thai or Khmer heritage site. Afternoon community service with Ban Phai district office — road-side tree planting along the main village road or school painting project (painting the school exterior mural). Service hours logged and witnessed by the district officer. Evening storytelling circle with a village elder through interpreter — students hear personal histories of the 1960s and 1980s migration waves that shaped Isan communities.

## Day 6 — Khon Kaen Museum & Farewell

Morning visit to Khon Kaen National Museum — guided tour of the Dvaravati-period artefacts (7th-11th century Buddha sculptures, bronze drums, pottery) and an exhibit on the ancient Khmer empire's influence on Isan. Lunch in Khon Kaen city at a local restaurant specialising in Isan cuisine — students taste som tam (green papaya salad), larb (minced meat salad), and khao neow (sticky rice). Afternoon coach transfer to Khon Kaen Airport. Evening flight back to Bangkok. Farewell dinner in Bangkok's Chinatown (Yaowarat) — students try pad thai from a street vendor and explore the gold shop-lined lanes.

## Day 7 — Departure Bangkok

Morning optional visit to Chatuchak Weekend Market (if a Saturday) or the Jim Thompson House silk museum. Students use their Thai language skills to negotiate a purchase at a market stall. Transfer to Suvarnabhumi Airport for onward travel.

## Curriculum fit

- **IB MYP Language & Culture**: sustained 7-day inquiry into Lao Phuan/Isan dialect versus central Thai — students produce a portfolio of linguistic observations (audio recordings, written journal entries, vocabulary flashcards)
- **IB DP Cultural Anthropology**: fieldwork component — Mekong community livelihoods study using participant observation methodology during the homestay
- **IGCSE Geography CIE Paper 2**: River fieldwork (Mekong River study) and community development case study (Ban Phai district) — both contribute to the Paper 2 structured questions
- **Bilingual program alignment**: dual-language activity sheets in Thai and English for all heritage site visits; students complete their reflection journal in both languages

## Safety

Local Isan coordinator present throughout Days 3-6 with daily morning check-in at the homestay cluster. Host family homestay pre-vetted through Khon Kaen University's community liaison office (12-point safety and facilities checklist). All homestays have western-style bathroom facilities, ceiling fans, and locking bedroom doors. Hospital partnership with Khon Kaen Regional Hospital (20-minute drive from Ban Phai). 24/7 trip director accessible by Thai mobile number provided to both students and host families before arrival. Students carry a pocket card with emergency contacts in Thai.

## What's included

Domestic flights (Bangkok-Khon Kaen return, economy class), all ground transport (coach, songthaew), twin-share accommodation (Bangkok boutique hotel 2 nights, homestay 4 nights with private room), all meals (daily breakfast, lunch, dinner), all activity fees and entrance tickets (Grand Palace, Wat Pho, Phimai, Khon Kaen Museum), local guide throughout Days 1-2 and 6, homestay orientation pack (prepared by Khon Kaen University), community service materials.
