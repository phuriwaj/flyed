## Day 1 — Arrival Chiang Mai

Arrive Chiang Mai International Airport. Transfer by air-conditioned coach to boutique hotel in old city, a 15-minute drive through the moat-lined streets. Welcome dinner at a family-run khao soi shop near the hotel — students try the northern Thai curry noodle dish and meet the shop owner's family. Evening briefing with the trip director: program overview, group norms, daily reflection expectations, and an introduction to the service partner organisations they will work with over the coming days.

## Day 2 — Service Orientation

Half-day cultural orientation session: temple etiquette (appropriate dress, photography rules, Wai Khru ritual), Thai language basics (sawadee krub/ka, khop khun krub/ka, chai/mai chai), and homestay expectations (family routines, meal times, bathroom facilities, gift protocol). Afternoon visit to the partner NGO office in the Chang Phuak district — staff present the community's background (riverine school serving 120 students from farming families), the ongoing infrastructure needs, and the role of international student groups in supplementing government funding. Group reflection circle: students share their initial impressions and identify personal learning goals for the week.

## Day 3 — Bamboo School Construction

Full-day service project at the partner school site in Mae Wang district, 45 minutes south of the city. Students work alongside Thai youth volunteers from the local university — cutting and treating bamboo poles, weaving wall panels, constructing a covered learning area that will serve as an outdoor classroom for the primary school. Tools (saws, hammers, drills) are provided; safety gloves and helmets are mandatory. Lunch is cooked on-site by the school's kitchen staff — students eat together with the children they are building for. Evening reflection session back at the hotel: what did the physical work teach you about the community you are serving? What would happen if international volunteers stopped coming?

## Day 4 — Hill-Tribe English Teaching

Travel by coach to a partner hill-tribe school in the Doi Sa Ket district (90-minute drive on winding mountain roads). Students lead a half-day English teaching session — structured around vocabulary games, a song session, and paired conversation practice with local primary students. The Thai teacher observes and provides feedback on pronunciation. Cultural exchange activity: students teach a short game from their home country and learn a Karen or Lahu traditional game in return. Return to Chiang Mai in the late afternoon. Evening free — students explore the Night Bazaar or the Sunday Walking Street market independently.

## Day 5 — Travel to Chiang Rai

Morning coach journey to Chiang Rai (3 hours north through mountain scenery on Route 118). Afternoon at the ethical elephant sanctuary — these are welfare-first sanctuaries where elephants are retired from tourism work. Students observe the elephants in a forest environment from a safe distance, learn about each elephant's rescue story from the keeper team, and assist with food preparation (cutting watermelon, pumpkin, and banana stems). There is no riding and no performances. Students can observe but not direct the elephants — this is their environment, not a show. Evening discussion on the ethics of elephant tourism across Thailand and how to evaluate sanctuary claims.

## Day 6 — Golden Triangle & Reflection

Morning visit to the Golden Triangle viewpoint — the confluence of Thailand, Myanmar, and Laos where the Mekong and Ruak rivers meet. Students observe the opium poppy fields (now legally cultivated with government compensation programs) and discuss the history of the Golden Triangle in the global narcotics trade. Afternoon workshop facilitated by a local historian on the transformation of the regional economy from opium to tourism to legal agriculture — students analyse economic data and draw their own conclusions. Final reflection circle: students share their most significant personal insight from the week and how their understanding of northern Thailand has changed. Group photo at the White Temple (Wat Rong Khun) on the return drive to Chiang Rai city.

## Day 7 — Departure

Morning visit to the Chiang Mai morning market (Warorot Market) — students navigate the fresh produce, textile, and spice sections, using Thai language phrases learned during the week to buy a souvenir for themselves. Transfer to Chiang Mai International Airport for onward travel. Students depart with a completed service log, reflection journal, and a certificate of participation.

## Curriculum fit

- **IB MYP Service & Action**: 18 verified service hours across bamboo construction (8h), English teaching (4h), and sanctuary observation with reflection (6h) — students present their Service & Action journal for MYP completion assessment
- **IB DP CAS**: counts toward the Creativity/Activity/Service component — the English teaching session and the construction project provide direct evidence of service engagement and sustained reflection
- **IGCSE Global Citizenship**: completed portfolio template — students submit a reflective written piece on the intersection of cultural understanding and service, framed by their hill-tribe school experience
- **GCSE**: contributes to the 'community involvement' component valued by UK secondary school leavers' university applications

## Safety

Daily safety brief before every activity — covering construction site hazards (tools, bamboo splinters), road travel (seatbelts, motion sickness prevention), and elephant sanctuary protocols (minimum 10m distance, no flash photography). 24/7 trip director with Thai mobile phone. Pre-departure risk assessment PDF shared with school coordinators 30 days before travel. All transport uses air-conditioned coaches with seatbelts. Hotel has 24-hour reception and safe deposit boxes. Hospital partnership with Chiang Mai Ram Hospital (10-minute drive from hotel). Vetted homestay families (for longer programs). Travel insurance with overseas emergency medical evacuation included.
