## Day 1 — Arrival Bangkok & Ayutthaya

Depart Bangkok for Ayutthaya by chartered coach (90 minutes). Morning guided tour of Wat Mahathat — the famous Buddha head embedded in banyan tree roots, the central prang towers, and the architectural layout of the 14th-century royal palace grounds. The guide explains the Ayutthaya period (1351-1767) as a hub of international trade — Portuguese, Dutch, Japanese, and Persian merchants. Afternoon at Wat Chaiwatthanaram on the west bank — students do a timed sunset sketching session, capturing the Portuguese-influenced prang design. Evening riverfront dinner — students sample boat noodles (kuay teow ruea) from the same spot where foreign merchants once traded.

## Day 2 — Ayutthaya Archaeological Day

Morning visit to the Ayutthaya Historical Study Centre with a curator-led exhibition walk — students analyse original chroniclestexts (Phra Cham etc.) and three-dimensional models of the city's layout before its destruction by Burmese forces in 1767. Afternoon cyclo tour of lesser-known temples: Wat Kuuk (the Chinese-style assembly hall), Wat Phra Kaew (reconstruction of the original Emerald Buddha site), and Wat Ratchaburana with its intact interior Buddha statues. Small-group object-analysis workshop using primary source documents — students interpret Ayutthaya-period chronicles to answer the inquiry question: how do we know what we know about ancient Siam? Evening facilitated discussion on ASEAN heritage frameworks and the tension between tourism revenue and preservation obligations at World Heritage sites.

## Day 3 — Travel to Kanchanaburi

Morning departure for Kanchanaburi (2.5-hour drive northwest). Arrive at the Hellfire Pass Memorial and Interpretive Centre — walk the 4km compressed railway cutting with a local historian who specialises in Australian and British POW accounts. Students follow the original railway alignment, reading name plaques at tunnel entrances. Afternoon at the Thai-Burma Railway Centre museum in Kanchanaburi town — structured student worksheet on forced labour demographics, mortality rates, and the construction timeline. Evening reflection circle beside the River Kwai — facilitated discussion on historical empathy and the ethics of wartime memory.

## Day 4 — Death Railway & Erawan Falls

Morning board the Death Railway for the scenic segment from Kanchanaburi to Nam Tok — the train passes through forested mountains, across concrete bridges, and through the three original tunnels still in use. At Nam Tok station, students walk to the former unloading platform and memorial. Visit the Allied POW cemetery in Kanchanaburi town — students lay flowers and read aloud one named individual's recorded service history. Afternoon hike to Erawan Falls (Tier 4 waterfall) — ecosystem study at each waterfall tier (bryophyte communities, fish species in plunge pools), water quality testing kits (DO, pH, temperature at three stations). Evening group dinner — optional documentary screening of a POW grandson's documentary.

## Day 5 — River Kwai Bridge & Living History

Morning walk across the iconic Bridge over the River Kwai (built 1943) with local guide. Students inspect the different rail sections and examine period photographs from the museum. Visit a traditional Mon village near the river — the Mon people are an ethnic minority indigenous to the region with their own language and weaving traditions; students participate in a brief weaving demonstration. Afternoon craft workshop: students produce a collaborative mural inspired by WWII-era Australian / British propaganda poster aesthetics, using locally sourced pigments and rice paper. Evening guest speaker — either a local historian from the Hellfire Pass Memorial or a descendant of forced labour, via video link.

## Day 6 — Return to Bangkok & Departure

Morning return drive to Bangkok (2.5 hours). Visit the Bangkok War Memorial on the river — WWII gallery with artefacts, uniforms, and a detailed account of the Thai government's position during the war. Students compare Thai and Allied perspectives using primary source documents provided. Farewell lunch near the Grand Palace — students receive their completed portfolio of worksheets and reflection journals. Transfer to Suvarnabhumi Airport for onward travel.

## Curriculum fit

- **IB MYP Individuals & Societies**: criterion A (knowing and understanding) — students analyse primary source chronicles to construct an evidence-based argument about Ayutthaya's trade networks
- **IB DP History**: Paper 2 (authoritarian states, WWII in Southeast Asia) and the IA investigation component — students formulate an IA research question on either the Thai-Burma Railway or Ayutthaya's urban morphology during the session
- **IGCSE History CIE Paper 2**: Depth study option on the Second World War in Asia — Hellfire Pass and POW experience fulfil the depth study requirements
- **GCSE History Edexcel**: Option 11 (War and the transformation of British society, including Asian theatres) — kanchanaburi case study maps to this option

## Safety

Licensed historical guide present at all WWII and heritage sites. Hospital partnership with Kanchanaburi Regional Hospital (10-minute drive from hotel). All coach travel uses seatbelt-equipped vehicles. Hellfire Pass trail is paved but has steep sections and uneven surfaces — age-appropriate pacing enforced, water bottles mandatory. Travel insurance with war-history site inclusions pre-reviewed before departure. All group activities at WWII sites include a designated meeting point and headcount protocol.
