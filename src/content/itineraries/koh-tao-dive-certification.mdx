## Day 1 — Arrival Koh Tao

Arrive Surat Thani Airport. Shared air-conditioned ferry transfer to Koh Tao (2.5 hours) — the Lomprayah catamaran departs from the mainland pier and students can watch the Gulf of Thailand coastline recede. Check into beachside dive resort on Sairee Beach — rooms open directly onto the sand. Afternoon equipment fitting session — students are sized for BCD (buoyancy control device), regulator, mask, snorkel, and booties. The PADI instructor leads a pool introduction in the resort's training pool: students familiarise themselves with underwater breathing, equalisation techniques, and the buddy check procedure (BWRAF). Evening welcome dinner at a beachfront restaurant and Dive Theory introduction — students receive the PADI Open Water manual and begin reading Chapter 1 on the physics of diving.

## Day 2 — Confined Water Sessions 1 & 2

Morning Dive Theory workshop — the instructor covers the key physics concepts required for the PADI exam: Boyle's law (pressure-volume relationship during descent/ascent), Henry's law (gas solubility and nitrogen absorption), Archimedes' principle (buoyancy control), and the no-decompression limit calculation. Students complete the knowledge review questions at the end of each chapter. Afternoon confined water session 1 in the resort pool: mask clearing (removing and replacing underwater), regulator clearing (exhaling through nose to clear water), and underwater breathing from a completely flooded mask. Confined water session 2: buoyancy control (hovering at 1.5m depth), fin kick variations (flutter kick, frog kick), and horizontal trim position. Evening beach volleyball and group dinner — students log their first confined water session in their PADI logbook.

## Day 3 — Confined Water Sessions 3 & 4

Morning briefing and confined water session 3: regulator recovery (retrieving a dropped regulator from the air hose), weight removal (controlled weight drop at the surface and underwater), and simulated safety stop practice (3-minute ascent pause at 5m). Confined water session 4: full circuit skills review — the instructor runs each of the five required performance requirements for Open Water Diver certification: mask removal and replacement, regulator recovery and clearing, air-sharing (buddy breathing), underwater mask clearing, and controlled swimming ascent. Each student is assessed against the PADI performance requirements. Afternoon Dive Theory workshop — marine biology basics: coral identification (hard coral genera: Acropora, Porites, Favia; soft coral types), fish family recognition (butterflyfish, angelfish, parrotfish, moray eels), and the ecological role of herbivores in reef health. Evening logbook review with the PADI instructor — students complete their knowledge reviews for chapters 1-4 and receive feedback.

## Day 4 — Open Water Dives 1 & 2

Morning boat departure from Sairee Beach pier to dive sites around Koh Tao's western and southern reefs. Dive 1 at Japanese Gardens (shallow reef, 6-8m) — students complete the five required open-water skills: underwater swim (200m or 300m with mask), compass navigation (one direction, return to starting point),buoyancy control (5m for 5 minutes), air-share ascent, and safety stop compliance. Dive 2 at Aow Leuk (8-12m) — swim-throughs in bommie formations, moray eels in crevices, octopus sightings common. Surface interval with tropical fruit and water on the boat deck. Afternoon debrief on underwater navigation using a compass and natural references (sun angle, reef formations). Evening open-water dive planning workshop — students plan Dive 3 using dive tables (no computer) and explain their reasoning to the instructor.

## Day 5 — Open Water Dives 3 & 4 & Certification

Morning Dive 3 at Chumpon Pinnacle (deeper dive, 18-22m for qualified students; shallower sites used for students still building confidence) — observe shark species (whitetip reef shark, sometimes nurse shark), giant trevally schools, and barra cuda. Dive 4 at Sail Rock (if conditions allow; otherwise Shark Island) — buoyancy mastery at varying depths, air consumption review, and final skill assessment. Afternoon pool session to review any outstanding skills from the PADI performance requirements. Evening certification ceremony on the beach — the PADI instructor presents certification cards, students sign their logbooks, and the group celebrates with a beachfront dinner at a seafood restaurant. Students who wish to continue to PADI Advanced Open Water can discuss the referral process with the instructor.

## Day 6 — Island Exploration & Departure

Morning free for snorkelling at Sairee Beach or a final shore dive (one-tank) at Twin Rocks before checkout. Optional pre-departure Dive Theory exam review session — the instructor runs through the 50-question exam format and students self-assess their readiness for the written exam. Lunch in Koh Tao town at a Thai restaurant near the pier. Afternoon ferry departure to mainland (2.5 hours). Transfer to Surat Thani Airport or onward bus to Chumphon for rail connections to Bangkok.

## Curriculum fit

- **IB MYP Physical & Health Education**: criterion B (planning) through dive planning and execution — students plan two dives independently using dive tables; criterion C (application) through skill performance underwater and sustained technique improvement across four open-water dives
- **IB DP CAS**: sustained activity component — students demonstrate personal challenge and skill acquisition in a new sport over 6 days, with evidence portfolio (logbook, instructor report, self-reflection)
- **IGCSE Physical Education CIE**: demonstrates physical literacy in an alternative sport environment; students submit a 500-word analysis of the physiological demands of scuba diving compared to other sports
- **A-Level Biology**: practical endorsement — direct observation of coral and fish ecology during open-water dives can contribute to the required CPAC (common practical assessment criteria) evidence for aquatic organism study

## Safety

PADI-certified instructor present on every boat dive, with a divemaster assistant for groups over 8 students. Maximum 4 students per instructor in open water. All dive equipment is serviced and inspected before the season begins and after every significant use. Safety sausage and DSMB (delayed surface marker buoy) carried on every dive. First-aid kit and emergency oxygen tank (on every boat). Swim test required before pool sessions — minimum 200m continuous swimming and 10-minute treading water. Medical questionnaire completed before booking — students with asthma, cardiac conditions, or ear/sinus issues require a doctor-signed dive medical. All students are briefed on decompression sickness symptoms and the nearest recompression chamber location (Bangkok Hospital Samui, 45-minute speedboat from Koh Tao).
