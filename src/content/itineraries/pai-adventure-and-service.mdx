## Day 1 — Arrival Pai

Arrive Chiang Mai Airport. Three-hour coach journey north to Pai on the winding Pai Valley Road (762 curves — students keep a curve count as an icebreaker). Check into Pai backpacker lodge with mountain views. Evening walk through Pai Night Market. Welcome dinner and program briefing.

## Day 2 — Canyon Hike & Pam Bok Waterfall

Morning hike through Pai Canyon — natural sandstone formations, narrow ridge trails, views over the valley. Afternoon visit to Pam Bok waterfall — swimming hole at the base. Community bike ride along rural lanes to a small Lahu village. Evening bonfire and reflection circle.

## Day 3 — Service at Community School

Full morning at a partner primary school in Pai district — students lead English language activities (games, songs, conversation circles). Afternoon environmental activity: students build and install composting bins or construct a small herb garden at the school. Service hours logged. Evening relaxation at Pai hot springs — natural mineral pools beside the river.

## Day 4 — Mountain Biking & Land Split

Morning mountain bike hire and guided ride on dirt trails through farmland and forest — 15km route with river crossings and hill sections suitable for beginners. Afternoon visit to the Land Split — geological curiosity where a hillside split during the 2008 earthquake. Guided explanation of tectonic activity in the region. Evening cooking class in Pai town — learn to make khao soi and sai ua from a local cook.

## Day 5 — Lod Cave & Pai River Rafting

Morning visit to Lod Cave — guided tour of the vast cavern with thousands of swiftlet nests. Students learn about the edible bird's nest industry and its economic role in the Pai district. Afternoon bamboo rafting on the Pai River — 8km float through jungle scenery with gentle rapids. Evening farewell dinner at a riverside restaurant. Student presentations on their favourite moment of the trip.

## Day 6 — Memorial Bridge & Departure

Morning short trek to Memorial Bridge — World War II-era bridge built by Allied POW labour (similar construction to the Kanchanaburi bridge). Historical explanation from local guide. Return to Pai town for free time and souvenir shopping. Afternoon coach transfer back to Chiang Mai (3 hours). Evening flight or overnight from Chiang Mai Airport.

## Curriculum fit

- **IB MYP Service & Action**: 12 verified service hours at community school and environmental installation
- **IB DP CAS**: sustained activity component — outdoor adventure skill progression in mountain biking and rafting
- **IGCSE Global Citizenship**: community development case study component
- **A-Level Geography**: individual investigation — tectonic landscape feature (Land Split) or river dynamics on the Pai River

## Safety

Certified outdoor guide accompanies all adventure activities. Mountain bikes serviced before each trip. Life jackets worn for all water activities. Bamboo rafting led by licensed river guide. Pai hot springs visited in designated safe zones. First-aid kit carried on all outdoor activities. Nearest hospital is in Mae Hong Son (45-minute drive from Pai). 24/7 trip director.

## What's included

All ground transport (coach Chiang Mai-Pai return), accommodation (Pai lodge, twin-share, air-conditioned), all meals, mountain bike hire (full-suspension), hot springs entry, Lod Cave entry, bamboo raft hire, school service materials, cooking class, all guide fees.
