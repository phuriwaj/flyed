## Day 1 — Arrival Chiang Mai

Arrive Chiang Mai International Airport. Host families meet students at the airport. Transfer to homestay households in Chiang Mai's Hang Dong orSantitham district. Welcome dinner with host family. Evening orientation walk with homestay coordinator.

## Day 2 — Language Assessment & Orientation

Morning at the language centre for Thai language assessment (written and spoken). Students placed in appropriate class level (beginner to intermediate). Afternoon cultural orientation session — Thai greeting customs (wai), dining etiquette, household expectations. Evening with host family.

## Day 3 — First Language Lessons

Morning Thai lesson 1: script introduction (consonant classes, vowels, tones). Lesson 2: basic conversation — introductions, asking for directions, numbers. Afternoon market visit with teacher — students practice buying fruit and vegetables in Thai. Evening host family dinner and vocabulary review.

## Day 4 — Temple Immersion Day

Morning visit to Wat Umong (forest temple) with Buddhist monk conversation session — students ask questions through interpreter about meditation and Thai Buddhist practice. Afternoon Thai lesson 3: vocabulary related to temple vocabulary and ordinal numbers. Evening with host family.

## Day 5 — Service at Local School

Morning language lesson. Afternoon visit to a partner primary school in the Doi Saket district — students assist with basic English activities and practice their Thai with primary school children. Debrief session on language learning strategies.

## Day 6 — Thai Cooking Class

Full morning at Thai cooking school — students cook four dishes (khao soi, som tam, pad thai, mango sticky rice) using Thai language instructions throughout. Afternoon language lesson focusing on food vocabulary and market transactions. Evening dinner with host family.

## Day 7 — Free Day with Host Family

Full day with host family — no scheduled activities. Students are encouraged to spend the day entirely in Thai. Optional group gathering in the evening for sharing and reflection.

## Day 8 — Intermediate Lessons & City Visit

Morning language lesson at higher level: sentence construction, question forms. Afternoon guided walk through Chiang Mai's old city — visit to Wat Chiang Man, Tha Phae Gate, Sunday Walking Street market (student uses Thai to negotiate a purchase). Evening with host family.

## Day 9 — Doi Suthep Excursion

Full-day excursion to Doi Suthep temple. Morning hike up the 309 steps with local guide speaking Thai throughout. Afternoon language lesson at a hilltop viewpoint — descriptive vocabulary and weather terms. Picnic lunch on the mountain. Evening return to host family.

## Day 10 — Language Lessons & Film

Morning intensive Thai lesson: reading comprehension exercise using Thai newspaper headlines. Afternoon Thai film screening with subtitles — followed by group discussion in Thai. Evening with host family.

## Day 11 — Service & Language

Morning language lesson. Afternoon return to partner school for a second session — student-led simple games in English and Thai. Reflection circle on cross-cultural communication challenges. Evening host family dinner.

## Day 12 — Chiang Rai Day Trip

Full-day excursion to Chiang Rai — visit White Temple (Wat Rong Khun), Blue Temple, and the Night Bazaar. Students practice Thai with local vendors and tour guides. Return to Chiang Mai in the evening.

## Day 13 — Advanced Lessons & Farewell Prep

Morning advanced Thai lesson: formal register, telephone language, writing a formal letter. Afternoon preparation for farewell ceremony — students prepare a short speech in Thai. Evening farewell dinner with host families and language centre teachers.

## Day 14 — Farewell & Departure

Morning farewell ceremony at the language centre — student presentations in Thai, certificate presentation, group photo. Final lunch with host families. Transfer to Chiang Mai Airport for onward travel.

## Curriculum fit

- **IB MYP Language & Culture**: sustained 14-day immersion — portfolio of language reflections, audio recordings, written journal entries
- **IB DP Language B ab initio**: fulfills the recommended 60 hours of contact time for Thai language acquisition
- **IGCSE First Language Thai (CIE)**: natural progression pathway — students can sit IGCSE Thai after the homestay
- **AP Chinese Language & Culture**: comparative language learning methodology applied to tonal language acquisition
- **Bilingual program**: dual-language journaling option for bilingual Thai students

## Safety

Homestay families vetted through a 12-point checklist by the language centre coordinator. Weekly coordinator visits to each homestay. 24/7 mobile number for student and family access to flyed coordinator. Hospital partnership with Chiang Mai Ram Hospital. All students complete a pre-departure medical form. Travel insurance with homestay cover included.

## What's included

14 nights homestay (private room, shared household), all meals with host family, 30 hours of Thai language tuition, all excursions and entrance fees, cooking class, language centre materials and certificate, Chiang Rai day trip transport, 24/7 coordinator support, all tips and donations at temples.
