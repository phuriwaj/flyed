## Day 1 — Arrival Phuket

Arrive Phuket International Airport. Transfer by minibus to beachside research station near Rawai village, five minutes from Chalong Bay. Welcome briefing from the resident marine biologist guide — students receive field notebooks, waterproof data sheets, and a waterproof camera pouch. Late afternoon orientation walk along Nai Harn beach to identify coastal vegetation and wave patterns. Group dinner at a southern Thai seafood restaurant in Rawai, where the guide introduces local fish species on the menu and explains sustainable fishing practices in the Andaman.

## Day 2 — Coral Reef Survey

Early morning boat departure from Chalong Bay to the reefs around Koh Racha Yai. Students conduct belt-transect coral cover surveys using standard Reef Check methodology — 50m transect lines, coral identification to genus level, and substrate percentage estimation. The data is entered into the Reef Check global database. Afternoon lab session at the Phuket Marine Biological Centre — specimen identification under microscope, coral bleaching index scoring, and data entry into spreadsheets. Evening lecture on coral bleaching mechanisms, ocean acidification chemistry, and the climate projection models for the Andaman Sea through 2050.

## Day 3 — Krabi & Mangrove Ecology

Travel by minibus to Krabi (90 minutes). Morning kayak through Ao Thalane mangrove channels — students navigate between buttress roots, identify Rhizophora and Avicennia species, and document fish diversity in tidal pools using seine net observation (catch-and-release). Afternoon visit to Tiger Cave Temple (Wat Tham) — climb 1,237 steps to the summit viewpoint overlooking the Andaman coastline and Krabi's famous limestone karst islands, with guide explanation of the temple's Buddhist history and the local传说. Evening bonfire discussion on coastal conservation policy — students debate marine protected area boundaries using real case studies from Maya Bay and the Similan Islands.

## Day 4 — Sea Turtle Conservation & Koh Phi Phi Ley

Visit the Phuket Sea Turtle Conservation Centre at Mai Khao Beach. Students assist rangers with hatchling feeding routines, learn about nest monitoring telemetry equipment, and record data on curved carapace measurements for the national turtle database. Afternoon guided ferry to Koh Phi Phi Ley — guided walk to Maya Bay (managed entry, max 30 minutes on shore per group) where students observe coral recovery since the 2018 closure and compare real visitor impact data against pre-closure estimates. Snorkel at Pileh Lagoon afterward, observing sergeant major fish and blacktip reef sharks in the shallows. Evening debrief session on marine protected area governance, student-led discussion on the merits of seasonal closures versus permanent protection.

## Day 5 — Departure Phuket

Morning visit to Phuket's Old Phuket Town — Sino-Portuguese shophouse architecture along Thalang Road, the historic Perananthong Market, and a stop at a local cafe for Phuket-style kaya toast. Students complete their field data summaries in pairs. Transfer to Phuket International Airport for onward travel.

## Curriculum fit

- **IB MYP Sciences**: criterion B (processing data) through statistical analysis of coral cover percentages across transects; criterion C (connecting inferences to scientific knowledge) by linking field observations to climate projection data
- **IB DP Environmental Systems & Societies**: fieldwork requirement fulfilled — mangrove ecosystem fieldwork and reef ecosystem survey contribute two separate IA fieldworkappendices
- **IGCSE Biology Paper 4 (CIE)**: fulfills the written alternative to practical paper requirement through supervised ecosystem fieldwork and formal data analysis write-up
- **AP Environmental Science**: contributes to units on aquatic ecology, conservation biology, and environmental policy — students produce a policy brief as assessment

## Safety

Certified marine biologist guide accompanies all water activities. Wetsuits, snorkels, booties, and scientific equipment provided. Daily safety brief before every water session — covering current speed, reef-safe sunscreen compliance, and jellyfish protocol. Coast guard weather checks conducted before all boat trips; itinerary adjusts for red-flag days. First-aid-trained staff on site 24/7. Emergency evacuation protocol to Phuket International SOS clinic (30-minute drive from Chalong Bay).
