## Day 1 — Arrival Khao Sok

Arrive Surat Thani Airport. Transfer by minibus to Khao Sok National Park headquarters (45-minute drive through rubber plantations and limestone cliffs). Check into the park guesthouse — rooms are simple but clean, with mosquito screens and ceiling fans. Lunch at the park guesthouse restaurant. Afternoon orientation nature walk on the 2km Riverside Trail — a certified park ranger guides plant identification (dipterocarp species, strangler figs, bamboo) and hornbill nesting observation. Evening slideshow presentation by the park ecologist on Khao Sok's natural history: how the 1980s dam project flooded the valley to create Cheow Wan Lake, transforming the ecosystem from lowland forest to a drowned limestone karst environment. Dinner and early night — the jungle is quiet after 9pm.

## Day 2 — Cheow Wan Lake & Freshwater Ecology

Early morning longtail boat across Cheow Wan Lake — the surreal flooded limestone cavern surrounded by 100m-tall karst cliffs. Students conduct freshwater quality testing at three lake stations using professional-grade kits: pH (indicator strip method), dissolved oxygen (Winkler titration), turbidity (Secchi disk), and temperature. Data is recorded on waterproof field sheets and later entered into a spreadsheet for analysis. Afternoon jungle hike to a remote viewing platform at the lake's eastern arm — students observe the successional vegetation zones from shoreline to summit and use binoculars for wildlife scanning (white-handed gibbon troops visible most mornings). Night safari by flat-bottomed boat — headtorches reveal eyeshine from owls, flying lemurs, and the occasional slow loris on the water's edge.

## Day 3 — Wildlife Tracking & Camera Traps

Morning ecology lecture on mammal tracking — footprint identification in sand patches (tiger, leopard cat, sun bear, gaur), scat analysis (diet reconstruction), and dung beetle indicators. Students deploy motion-sensor camera traps at three pre-selected locations: one near a wildlife trail, one at a mineral lick, one near a stream crossing. Data collection lesson using distance sampling methodology — students establish a 500m transect line, record encounter rates, and calculate relative abundance indices. Afternoon visit to a Rafflesia site (seasonal — visible December through February; in other months, an alternative rainforest understory plant study is substituted) — the park naturalist explains the parasitic flowering biology of Rafflesia kerrii and the specific host vine (Tetrastigma). Evening group reflection circle — students discuss the conservation challenges facing Khao Sok: illegal logging, wildlife trade, tourism pressure, and climate-driven habitat changes.

## Day 4 — Mangrove River & Community

Morning kayak on the Khao Sok River through a mixed mangrove forest — students identify mangrove species (Rhizophora apiculata, Avicennia alba, Bruguiera gymnorhiza) using field guides and record their zonation pattern relative to the waterline. Fish trap installations (lucky nets) used by local communities are observed and their catch-and-release protocol is explained by a community member. Afternoon visit to a local rubber plantation — the tapper demonstrates sustainable harvesting techniques and explains the carbon sequestration economics of rubber agroforestry versus oil palm monoculture. Community conversation with a forest patrol ranger from the Khao Sok National Park protection unit — students hear directly about illegal logging detection, patrolling methods, and recent confiscation cases. Farewell dinner with the park rangers and flyed ecologist guide.

## Day 5 — Departure

Morning guided birding walk along the 3km nature trail — over 180 bird species have been recorded in Khao Sok, including the endangered Malaysian rail-babbler, great argus pheasant, and white-crowned hornbill. The guide uses playback calls to attract skulking species. Final data summary session — students compile their five days of field observations (lake chemistry data, camera trap results, mangrove species list) into a group research poster. They vote on their most significant finding and prepare a 3-minute presentation. Transfer to Surat Thani Airport for onward travel.

## Curriculum fit

- **IB MYP Sciences**: criterion A (scientific inquiry) — students formulate a testable hypothesis about water quality or wildlife activity before the trip; criterion B (data processing and evaluation) — statistical treatment of lake chemistry data and camera trap capture rates
- **IB DP Environmental Systems & Societies**: fieldwork requirement for both aquatic (lake) and terrestrial (jungle) ecosystems — students produce two separate IA fieldworkappendices from data collected
- **IGCSE Biology CIE Paper 5**: fulfills the alternative to practical paper requirement through supervised 5-day field work and a formal data analysis write-up
- **A-Level Geography**: individual investigation — rivers or ecosystems option; students can use primary data from lake chemistry sampling or mangrove transect for their 3,000-word independent investigation

## Safety

Park-certified nature guide accompanies all activities. Life jackets provided and mandatory for all water activities. Jungle trails assessed daily for safety — land leeches are more common during the wet season (June-October); gaiters are provided on request. First-aid kit carried on every hike. Khao Sok National Park ranger station is 15 minutes from any trailhead by boat or road. Nearest hospital is in Phang Nga town (45-minute drive from park headquarters). All students complete a medical form before booking — any student with severe allergies carries an epinephrine auto-injector provided by the trip director.
