## Day 1 — Arrival Bangkok

Arrive Suvarnabhumi Airport. Transfer to hotel near Lumpinee area — the heart of Bangkok Muay Thai. Evening welcome dinner. Introduction to the program, trip director briefing, Muay Thai rules and etiquette session.

## Day 2 — Muay Thai Fundamentals

Morning technical training at a licensed Bangkok camp — stance, jab, cross, elbow blocks with certified coach. Afternoon active recovery: light jog along the Chao Phraya riverbank and stretching session. Evening visit to Lumpinee Stadium — observe professional bouts (appropriate seating, student debrief after).

## Day 3 — Technical Training & Temple Visit

Morning second training session — introduce kick and knee techniques. Afternoon visit to Wat Arun and Wat Pho — students explore the martial arts iconography in temple reliefs and compare them to modern Muay Thai techniques. Evening discussion on the role of sport in Thai identity and tourism economy.

## Day 4 — Travel to Chiang Mai & Service Orientation

Morning flight to Chiang Mai (1h 10m). Afternoon service orientation with local NGO partner — workshop on the community being served (youth after-school program in a peri-urban district). Cultural sensitivity briefing and Thai language basics for working with children.

## Day 5 — Community Coaching

Full day at partner youth centre. Students lead structured Muay Thai warm-up sessions and basic pad work with local children under coach supervision. Service hours logged. Afternoon co-design activity — groups plan a 30-minute activity for the children for Day 6. Evening reflection circle and northern Thai dinner.

## Day 6 — Service Delivery & Temple Run

Morning at youth centre — students deliver their planned activity sessions. Afternoon release session at a local track — short distance running, agility circuits, team games. Evening visit to Doi Suthep temple at golden hour — jog or songthaew up the mountain road, temple explanation from local guide.

## Day 7 — Doi Inthanon Hike & Closing Ceremony

Morning drive to Doi Inthanon (Thailand's highest peak, 2,565m). Moderate 3km jungle trail hike to the summit area — views, endemic flora, royal memorials. Afternoon return to Chiang Mai city. Closing ceremony with youth centre children and families. Farewell dinner at night market.

## Day 8 — Departure Chiang Mai

Morning free for market shopping or optional early-morning temple meditation. Transfer to Chiang Mai Airport for onward travel.

## Curriculum fit

- **IB MYP Physical & Health Education**: criterion B (planning) and criterion C (application and performance) — students plan and deliver coaching sessions to peers
- **IB DP CAS**: sustained service action component — 20+ hours coaching and community engagement
- **IGCSE Physical Education Edexcel**: analysis of skill transfer from Muay Thai training to broader physical literacy
- **AP Psychology**: unit on behavioral learning (observational learning/pad work coaching technique)

## Safety

Muay Thai training supervised by certified coach at all times — all pad work and sparring optional and consent-based. Headgear and gloves required for all contact drills. Youth centre service activities supervised by flyed staff and local NGO staff. Doi Inthanon hike has a certified guide and is kept to marked trails. 24/7 trip director. Hospital partnerships in both Bangkok and Chiang Mai.

## What's included

Domestic flights (Bangkok-Chiang Mai return), city transport and coach, accommodation (Bangkok hotel near Muay Thai camp, Chiang Mai boutique hotel), all meals, Muay Thai training fees (coach, gloves, pads, wrap), youth centre service materials, temple entrance fees, Doi Inthanon park fees and guide.
