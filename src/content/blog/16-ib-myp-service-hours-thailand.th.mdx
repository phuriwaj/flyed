IB MYP service learning is not volunteer tourism. The distinction matters for IB validation, and it matters for educational integrity. A properly structured Thailand program can deliver genuine service hours that meet IB criteria — but the structure has to be in place before departure.

## The IB MYP service requirements

IB MYP students must complete at least 15 hours of service over the programme duration (typically years 4 and 5 of secondary school). This is not an attendance requirement — it is a learning outcome requirement. Students must demonstrate that they have engaged with a community, understood the issues at stake, taken action, and reflected on the experience.

The service cycle — investigation, planning, action, reflection — is the framework. Every activity must fit this cycle. Simply showing up and helping is not sufficient for IB credit.

## What documentation IB requires

The MYP service journal (or e-portfolio) must contain:

- Evidence of investigation: research into the community issue being addressed
- Evidence of planning: how the student prepared for their service
- Evidence of action: what the student did, with dates and locations
- Evidence of reflection: structured reflections at multiple points during and after the service

Documentation must be contemporaneous — reflections written weeks after the experience are not considered valid evidence. Schools must have processes in place to collect student reflections during the trip itself.

## Structuring a Thailand program for MYP compliance

A typical two-week Thailand program can deliver MYP service hours in several ways:

**Direct service** — working in a community partner organization, such as a environmental conservation project, school support program, or community infrastructure work. Students must be doing genuine work, not observation.

**Indirect service** — raising funds or awareness for a Thai organization. Fundraising must benefit a specific organization with documented impact.

**Advocacy** — education campaigns, awareness materials, or research that supports a community cause.

The key IB requirement is that students must understand the issue before taking action. This means pre-departure research is not optional — it is part of the required investigation phase.

## CAS versus MYP service

IB Diploma Programme CAS (Creativity, Activity, Service) and MYP service share similar philosophical foundations but have different hour requirements and documentation standards. CAS requires 50 hours minimum across the diploma programme (typically 2 years), with at least one project that is student-led and runs for an extended period.

A Thailand trip can count toward CAS if the activity meets three criteria: it is genuine engagement (not superficial), it is documented throughout, and it includes a reflection component. Our CAS alignment pack covers these requirements separately from the MYP materials.

## Common IB audit failures and how to avoid them

The most common reasons schools fail IB service audits for Thailand trips:

- Student reflections are too generic ("I learned a lot about Thai culture")
- No evidence of student-led planning — students were told what to do
- Service project is charity tourism rather than genuine community engagement
- Hours documented but no learning outcome evidence
- No pre-departure investigation phase

We brief all schools on these failure modes before booking and provide guidance to ensure documentation is complete before the IB audit window.
