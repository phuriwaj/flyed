Koh Tao is one of the world's most concentrated dive certification locations. Thousands of people earn their PADI Open Water certification there every year, and school groups are increasingly including diving as part of Thailand itineraries. Here is what coordinators need to know before booking.

## Why Koh Tao

Koh Tao is small, safe, and purpose-built for dive training. The island has dive shops on every block, calm shallow bays for confined water sessions, and dive sites reachable within 20 minutes by boat. Certification costs are significantly lower than in Europe or Australia — typically $300 to $400 USD for a full PADI Open Water course — and the water is warm year round, removing the need for thick wetsuits.

For school groups, the island's small scale also simplifies logistics. Everything is walkable, transport between the dive shop and accommodation is straightforward, and medical support is available on-island.

## Age requirements and certifications

The PADI Open Water certification has a minimum age of 15. Students aged 10 to 14 can earn the PADI Junior Open Water certification, which is functionally identical but restricts dives to 18 metres maximum depth with adult supervision until age 15.

flyed's Koh Tao dive program accepts students aged 15 and above for full Open Water certification, and students aged 12 to 14 for Junior Open Water where the school curriculum and risk assessment allow.

Students under 15, or groups where some members cannot dive, can complete a Discover Scuba experience in a confined environment — this does not count as certification but gives students a genuine underwater experience under direct instructor supervision.

## Safety and medical considerations

Scuba diving carries inherent risks that require careful management in a school context. All participants must complete a PADI medical self-assessment form before in-water sessions. Conditions requiring medical clearance include asthma, heart conditions, diabetes, and ear or sinus issues. A physician's clearance is required for any condition that produces a "yes" response on the medical form.

flyed's dive program is delivered by PADI-certified dive centres with a minimum of one instructor per 4 students during all in-water sessions. All instructors hold EFR (Emergency First Response) certification and have experience working with school groups.

Students who cannot swim 200 metres continuously and float for 10 minutes are not permitted to complete Open Water certification. This is a PADI requirement, not a flyed policy.

## What the certification involves

The PADI Open Water course consists of three components: knowledge development (online or classroom), confined water sessions (swimming pool or shallow confined water), and open water dives (4 total).

On Koh Tao, the full course can be completed in 3 to 4 days. Day 1 covers knowledge development and confined water. Days 2 and 3 cover the four open water dives across two days.

flyed's school group itinerary typically runs the knowledge development component before arrival on Koh Tao — either as pre-departure study or during travel days — to maximise water time on the island.

## Connecting to curriculum

Open water diving connects naturally to biology (coral ecology, marine species), chemistry (gas laws, decompression), and physics (pressure, buoyancy). Students who complete certification leave with logbook entries and a permanent certification card. The certification is recognised worldwide and does not expire, though a refresher course is recommended after a gap of more than 12 months.

## Cost considerations

The certification fee covers instructor time, equipment rental, course materials, and dive site fees. Schools should budget separately for accommodation, food, and transport to Koh Tao (speedboat from Chumphon or ferry from Samui). flyed's itinerary includes all logistics from mainland Thailand.

The Junior Open Water certification is priced identically to full Open Water at most dive centres.
