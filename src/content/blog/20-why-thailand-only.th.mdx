flyed used to offer programs in six countries. Today we operate only in Thailand. The decision to narrow our geographic focus was not made for marketing reasons — it was made because we believe depth is better than breadth for the schools and students we serve.

## What geographic focus actually means

Running programs in one country means we know every partner organization personally. We have visited every camp, homestay family, dive operator, and service project we use. We know which guides speak excellent English and which ones do not. We know which hotels have reliable hot water and which ones do not.

This knowledge does not exist at scale. It accumulates through years of direct relationships, annual audits, and feedback from school groups. When we decided to maintain this knowledge at the standard our schools expect, operating across multiple countries became unsustainable.

## The school safety argument for depth

School trip safety depends on knowing your operating environment. In Thailand, we have developed safety protocols for every activity type we offer — from jungle treks to open-water diving to homestay accommodation. These protocols are documented, trained, and audited annually.

This documentation does not exist for other countries. Building it to the same standard would require years of in-country work and a dedicated safety team that we do not have. Until we can build that knowledge with the same rigor, we will not offer programs in other countries.

## Why Thailand specifically

Thailand offers a combination of factors that makes it the strongest destination for school programs targeting IB MYP service, CAS, and global citizenship curricula:

English is widely spoken in our operating areas, which allows students to engage directly with community partners without requiring interpreters. This changes the nature of the service — students can have genuine conversations with the people they are working alongside.

The NGO and community organization sector is mature. Chiang Mai alone has hundreds of organizations with established school partnership programs. We have vetted and audited every organization we work with.

The infrastructure is reliable. Road transport is well-developed, domestic flights are frequent and inexpensive, hospitals meet international standards in major cities, and telecommunications coverage extends across our operating regions.

The cost structure allows longer programs for the same budget. A two-week program in northern Thailand costs significantly less than equivalent programming in Europe or North America. More time in-country produces better learning outcomes.

## What this means for schools

Schools that book with flyed are booking with an operator that has deep local knowledge, established safety protocols, and direct relationships with every partner in the program. This is not a general principle — it is a specific operational reality.

When something goes wrong — and things do go wrong, on every trip, in every country — our in-country coordinator is a phone call away, knows the local emergency services, and has pre-established relationships with the hospitals and authorities we use.

That relationship takes years to build. We are not willing to compromise it by spreading our attention across multiple countries.

## What we are not saying

We are not saying Thailand is the only suitable destination for school programs. Other operators have built genuine depth in other countries, and schools should evaluate operators on their specific knowledge, not their geographic range.

We are also not saying that operators offering multiple countries cannot do so safely. Many do. We are saying that we made a deliberate choice to focus, and that choice benefits the schools we work with.
