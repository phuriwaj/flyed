Most marine biology trips involve snorkeling, a dolphin watch, and a lecture on coral bleaching that students forget by the airport. That is not a STEM field trip. That is a tourist itinerary with an educational label.

Here is how a well-designed Andaman coast program actually builds scientific skills.

## Why the Andaman coast works for STEM

The Andaman Sea off Koh Lanta and Koh Phi Phi sits at the intersection of two distinct marine ecosystems: the open ocean and the coastal reef. Water temperature, salinity, and current patterns vary enough within a 30-kilometre radius to give students real data to compare.

Coral reefs here show measurable bleaching stress patterns across depth gradients, which makes them ideal for hypothesis-driven fieldwork. Student data from our 2024 programs showed live coral cover ranging from 38% to 71% across six sites — enough variance to write a proper null hypothesis and test it.

## Structure for IB DP Biology and ESS

A field trip earns credit when students collect primary data they use in their internal assessment. For IB Biology, that means planning a research question, collecting raw data in the field, processing it with appropriate statistical tests, and discussing limitations. ESS adds evaluation of methodologies and stakeholder perspectives.

The Andaman program we run with marine-science partners is structured around three full research days:

- **Day 1:** Reef health survey using line-intercept transects at two depths. Students formulate their question on the boat before entering the water.
- **Day 2:** Water column analysis — dissolved oxygen, pH, nitrate, and temperature at surface and 5 metres. Includes a boat-based current and turbidity measurement exercise.
- **Day 3:** Data analysis workshop at the research station. Students process results in groups, run t-tests or ANOVA as appropriate, and draft their IA methodology section.

## What students take away

Beyond the IA data, students leave with field-skills certification from the Marine Research Foundation, including transect use, coral identification to genus level, and water-quality testing protocols. These are transferable skills they use in university lab work.

## What to look for in a marine STEM provider

Insist on raw data ownership — students must leave with their own dataset, not a processed summary. Verify the research station has laboratory space for data processing. Ask whether the program includes pre-departure methodology training or whether that time is eaten by logistics on arrival.

The difference between a trip that produces an IA and one that produces a photo album is mostly a question of how the first three days are sequenced.
