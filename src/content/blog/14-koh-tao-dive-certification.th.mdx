Koh Tao — Turtle Island — is the dive certification capital of Southeast Asia. More than 100,000 divers certify on its beaches every year, drawn by clear water, abundant marine life, and a competitive market that keeps prices low. For school groups, it offers a genuine alternative to classroom-only environmental education.

## Why dive certification belongs in a school travel program

Open-water diving is a lifetime skill, not a holiday activity. Students who certify on a school trip return home with a qualification they can use anywhere in the world. The certification cost is a fraction of what they would pay individually, and the group experience creates different social dynamics than recreational diving.

Beyond the qualification, the underwater environment teaches marine biology, conservation, and environmental science in ways that textbooks cannot replicate. Seeing a coral reef directly — the structure, the biodiversity, the visible impacts of warming — changes how students understand ocean ecosystems.

## Age requirements and certification options

The minimum age for full open-water certification (PADI or SSI) is 10 years old, with restrictions on depth and conditions for divers under 15. Most reputable operators apply additional restrictions for under-12s, including a maximum depth of 12 meters and diving only with a parent or guardian.

For school groups with mixed ages, we recommend the PADI Junior Open Water certification for eligible students and referral options for younger students who want to complete knowledge development and pool skills at school before returning to Koh Tao for open-water checkout dives at 15.

Medical clearance is required for all divers. Students with asthma, heart conditions, or ear problems need a physician-signed medical form. We provide the standard PADI medical questionnaire to all schools before booking so issues are identified early rather than on arrival.

## Selecting a dive operator on Koh Tao

Koh Tao has over 100 dive shops, and quality varies significantly. Our vetting criteria for school groups include: instructor-to-student ratio (maximum 1:4 for open-water skills sessions), equipment maintenance records, safety incident history, and student welfare protocols.

We work with three operators on Koh Tao that meet our standards: Crystal Dive (largest and most structured), Bans Dive (good for younger groups), and Ocean Sound Dive (specializes in smaller ratios). All three have dedicated school group programs with age-appropriate instruction.

## What the certification course covers

A PADI Open Water course takes four to five days. The academic portion covers dive physics, physiology, equipment, environment, and dive planning. Confined water sessions (pool or shallow sandy-bottom sites) develop the five core skills: mask clearing, regulator clearing, buoyancy control, air-sharing, and ascent procedures.

Open-water dives — four required — are completed at local dive sites around Koh Tao. Typical sites for student groups include Hin Wong (good for seeing turtles and rays), White Rock (shallow, calm, diverse marine life), and Twins (often has shark sightings).

## Physical fitness and swimming requirements

PADI requires that students can swim 200 meters or tread water for 10 minutes. These are minimum standards, and we encourage students to practice before arrival. Non-swimmers can participate in resort dives in shallow water but cannot receive full certification.

Decompression illness risk is low for shallow, no-decompression dives, but students are briefed on symptoms and the 24-hour no-fly rule after diving. We enforce the no-fly rule strictly — no student flies within 18 hours of their final dive.

## Cost breakdown

A group open-water certification on Koh Tao typically costs $350–$450 per student, including academic instruction, confined water sessions, open-water dives, equipment rental, and certification card. This is significantly below the $600–$800 charged in Europe or North America for equivalent courses.
