Packing for a Thailand school trip requires a different approach than packing for a European holiday. The climate, cultural context, and activity requirements are all different from what most students and parents expect. This guide covers exactly what students need and why.

## Clothing essentials

Thailand is hot and humid year-round. Temperatures range from 28 to 36 degrees Celsius in the lowlands and slightly cooler in the northern highlands. Humidity is consistently above 70%. Students who pack as if they are going to southern Europe will be uncomfortable.

**Every student needs:**

- Lightweight long trousers (2–3 pairs) — required for temple visits, mosquito protection, and sun protection. Denim is too heavy; lightweight cotton or linen blends are best.
- Knee-length shorts or skirts — for casual days and sports activities
- T-shirts and light shirts (4–5) — breathable, quick-dry fabrics preferred over cotton
- A light long-sleeve shirt or cover-up — for sun protection and temple visits
- A light waterproof jacket — the rainy season runs May to October; even outside the rainy season, afternoon downpours occur
- Swimwear (2 pieces for swimming and water activities)
- A hat with a wide brim or neck flap — essential for sun protection
- Comfortable walking shoes — closed-toe shoes with grippy soles for trekking and water activities
- Sandals or flip-flops — for casual wear and temple visits (temples require shoes to be removed; slides and flip-flops are easier to remove than sandals)

**Do not bring:** Jeans (too hot and heavy to dry), formal clothing (not needed), or anything you would be upset about ruining.

## Toiletries and health

- SPF 50+ sunscreen — significantly more expensive in Thailand and not always available in familiar brands
- Mosquito repellent with DEET — Thai mosquitoes are persistent; 30% DEET minimum recommended
- Lip balm with SPF
- Basic first aid kit — plasters, antiseptic, personal medications
- Any prescription medications in original packaging with doctor's letter
- Rehydration salts (ORS sachets) — widely available but useful to have on hand
- Hand sanitiser
- Any required vaccinations documentation

## Electronics

- Phone with international roaming enabled or a Thai SIM card — available at Bangkok airport on arrival
- Universal power adapter — Thai sockets are Type A/B (same as US) or Type C/F (European round pin)
- Power bank — useful for long travel days
- Headlamp — for trekking and cave activities
- Camera or phone — for journal and portfolio documentation

## Documents

- Passport (valid for 6 months beyond travel dates)
- Copy of passport information page stored separately
- Travel insurance certificate
- Any required vaccination records
- School trip information pack
- Emergency contact numbers

## Cultural and practical items

- A small daypack or shoulder bag — for daily excursions
- A sarong or light scarf — useful for temple visits, covering shoulders, and as an impromptu towel
- Water bottle — refillable; tap water is not potable in most areas
- Small amount of Thai baht cash — for markets and small purchases; ATMs are widely available
- A notebook and pen — for journaling during the trip

## What students commonly forget

Based on years of running school trips, the items students forget most often are: sufficient mosquito repellent, a hat, a universal power adapter, and a reusable water bottle. A week before departure, run through the packing list with your student and check each item.

## What parents should know

Students on school trips have different packing needs than students travelling independently. Clothes will get dirty. Items will be left behind. Build in a small budget for replacing forgotten items — Thai shopping centres have everything at reasonable prices, and a lost item replaced in Bangkok costs less than an emergency courier delivery from home.

The goal is for every student to arrive comfortable, prepared, and able to participate fully in every activity. Packing the right items in the right quantities is a significant part of making that happen.
