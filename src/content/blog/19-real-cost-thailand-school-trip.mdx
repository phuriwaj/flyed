School trip costs are one of the most common barriers to participation. A transparent breakdown of what a Thailand school trip actually costs — and where that money goes — helps coordinators, parents, and students plan effectively. This guide provides real numbers and practical fundraising approaches.

## What a Thailand school trip actually costs

Costs vary significantly based on itinerary, duration, group size, and the operator you choose. The following is a realistic range for a 10-day flyed program with a group of 16 students and 2 teachers, departing from Europe or North America.

**Flights:** Return international flights typically range from $800 to $1,400 USD per person depending on departure country and booking window. Booking 4–6 months in advance typically yields the best prices. Group bookings require 8+ weeks lead time and may carry a small booking fee.

**Program and operator fees:** flyed's complete program fee — covering accommodation, all activities, in-country transport, meals, and instructor ratios — ranges from $1,200 to $1,800 USD per student depending on itinerary and duration. This does not include flights.

**Accommodation:** For the homestay components of flyed itineraries, there is no accommodation cost — families host students as part of the program. Hotel accommodation for teacher supervisors is included in the program fee.

**Visas:** Most UK, EU, Australian, and US passport holders enter Thailand visa-free for 30 days. No visa cost for most school trip participants.

**Travel insurance:** Comprehensive school group travel insurance with medical evacuation coverage typically runs $40 to $80 USD per person for a 10-day trip. This is mandatory and should not be skipped.

**Vaccinations:** Routine vaccinations should be up to date. Hepatitis A and Typhoid are recommended for most travellers. Japanese Encephalitis is recommended for trips exceeding 4 weeks. Costs vary by country and healthcare provider.

**Pocket money:** Students typically spend $5 to $15 USD per day on snacks, souvenirs, and market purchases. $100 to $150 USD for the trip is a reasonable allowance.

**Equipment and clothing:** Budget $50 to $150 USD per student for appropriate clothing and gear if not already owned.

**Total per student (excluding flights):** approximately $1,400 to $2,100 USD.

## Why Thailand is cost-effective

Thailand offers a lower cost of living than Europe, North America, or Australia, which means in-country costs — accommodation, food, transport, activities — are significantly cheaper than equivalent programs in Western countries. A 10-day adventure sports program in Thailand costs substantially less than the same program delivered in the Scottish Highlands or Colorado.

The exchange rate advantage for USD, GBP, EUR, and AUD visitors is meaningful without being the primary reason to choose the destination. A meal at a local restaurant costs $2 to $5 USD. Private transport for a full day costs $30 to $50 USD. These costs compound across a 10-day itinerary.

## Fundraising strategies that work

Schools that achieve high participation rates in their Thailand trips typically run structured fundraising programs rather than leaving it to individual students. Here are approaches that have worked:

**Group fundraising events:** School dinners, quiz nights, sports tournaments, and cultural evenings can raise significant funds while building group cohesion before departure. Allocate the proceeds to a travel fund accessible to all participants.

**Sponsor a student program:** Some schools partner with a parent association or alumni network to partially subsidise students with demonstrated financial need. Anonymous subsidies are possible if the school prefers not to publicise which students receive support.

**Crowdfunding platforms:** dedicated platforms like FundMyTravel allow students to create personal fundraising pages with school trip context. The most successful campaigns combine personal stories with a clear description of the educational purpose.

**Grant applications:** Several foundations and trusts fund international educational experiences for students. The Rotary Club, local community foundations, and some corporate CSR programs are regular funders of school exchange and trip programs.

**Advance payment plans:** Allowing families to pay in instalments rather than a single lump sum reduces the financial barrier. A 6-month payment plan makes a $1,800 trip feel very different from a $1,800 bill.

## Making the case to parents

When presenting the trip to parents and governors, frame the cost in terms of value. A 10-day fully-supervised Thailand program with adventure sports, cultural immersion, and curriculum connections costs less per day than many residential trips within Europe. The question is not whether the trip is expensive — it is whether the investment is worth what students gain.
