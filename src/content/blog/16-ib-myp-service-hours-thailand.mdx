IB MYP students require documented service hours as part of their programme requirements. A well-structured Thailand trip can provide a meaningful proportion of those hours, particularly for students completing their personal project or working toward the MYP certificate. Here is how the connection works and what to look for when evaluating a trip provider.

## How MYP service hours work

MYP students are expected to engage in sustained service learning — not one-off volunteering, but activities that involve planning, action, and reflection over time. The requirement is not simply a tally of hours but evidence of genuine engagement with a community need, personal growth, and reflection on the impact of the student's actions.

Students in MYP years 3 and 4 (ages 13–15) complete a minimum of one significant service project, typically linked to their personal project. Students in MYP year 5 complete community service as part of the programme, with a minimum of 15 hours expected in many schools.

## How flyed's Thailand program connects to MYP requirements

flyed's Northern Thailand Service Week itinerary is specifically designed with MYP service learning in mind. The program is structured around three service projects that students prepare for, execute, and reflect on:

**Project 1: children's home support.** Students work at a partnered children's home in Chiang Mai for two days, engaging in structured activities — facility improvement, tutoring younger children, collaborative games — with a pre-briefing on the context and a structured reflection session afterward.

**Project 2: environmental conservation.** Students participate in reforestation or river cleanup activities in the Mae Taeng area. Data collection is included: students measure seedling survival rates or record waste collected, giving them quantitative evidence of impact.

**Project 3: elephant sanctuary engagement.** At a certified ethical elephant sanctuary, students observe and document welfare conditions, learn about mahout culture and the history of working elephants in Thailand, and assist with food preparation and habitat maintenance. This project has the deepest cultural learning component.

## Assessment and documentation

Each project is accompanied by a flyed service journal that students complete during the trip. The journal is structured to match MYP's service learning criteria:

- **Planning** — students state the need they are addressing and what they expect to contribute
- **Action** — students describe what they did, what happened, and what they observed
- **Reflection** — students write about what they learned about themselves, the community, and the issue

These journals are formatted for direct submission to school MYP coordinators. Students can use them as evidence for their personal project or service portfolio.

## Connecting to the personal project

The Thailand service program works particularly well as the action component of a personal project. A student researching access to education in northern Thailand could base their investigation on observations from the children's home visit. A student studying sustainable tourism could use the elephant sanctuary context as a primary case study.

flyed can provide post-trip supporting letters for students whose personal project references their trip experience, confirming participation and the nature of activities.

## What schools need to do

The school MYP coordinator should confirm before travel which activities will count toward service requirements and how many hours will be attributed to each component. flyed provides a full activity schedule with learning objectives and hour allocations at booking confirmation, which coordinators can use for pre-approval.

Schools requiring a formal partnership agreement or memorandum of understanding for credit purposes should request this at least 10 weeks before travel.
