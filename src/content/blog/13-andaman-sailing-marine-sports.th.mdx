The Andaman Sea coast of southern Thailand — Phuket, Krabi, the Phi Phi Islands, Koh Lanta — offers one of Southeast Asia's most varied marine environments. For school groups, a sailing and marine sports itinerary delivers outdoor skills, environmental education, and the logistical simplicity of a floating classroom.

## Why the Andaman works for school sailing programs

Thailand's Andaman coast has a well-established charter fleet with experienced captains, well-maintained vessels, and predictable seasonal weather patterns. The sailing window runs from November to April, with December through March offering the calmest conditions.

The route typically runs from Phuket or Ao Nang through the Phi Phi Islands, with overnight anchorages at Maya Bay, Koh Bida, and Koh Haa. These locations offer shelter from open-ocean swells, clear water for diving and snorkeling, and dramatic karst scenery that students find genuinely memorable.

## What students learn on a sailing week

No prior sailing experience is required. Our flotilla of three vessels carries an instructor-to-student ratio of 1:6, with professional skippers on each boat. Students rotate through sailing roles: helm, navigator, crew, and watch leader.

By the end of the week, most students can handle a boat under sail in moderate conditions, tie standard nautical knots, and understand basic navigation using paper charts and GPS. These are transferable skills — not just sailing skills.

The marine biology component runs alongside sailing instruction. Daily snorkeling sessions at coral reef sites are guided by our marine educator, who briefs students on reef ecology, coral identification, and the impact of ocean warming on Andaman reef systems. Water quality testing using professional monitoring kits is part of the curriculum.

## Kayaking, paddleboarding, and diving options

The Andaman itinerary can include kayaking through sea caves at Koh Phi Phi, paddleboarding in calm anchorages, and introductory scuba diving at shallow reef sites. Open-water diving certification is available as an add-on for students aged 15 and above who meet medical requirements.

Snorkeling is available to all students regardless of swimming ability, with buoyancy support provided. Non-swimmers are never required to enter deep water.

## Safety protocols on the water

Our sailing program operates under a formal safety management system. Each vessel carries a VHF radio, EPIRB, first-aid kit, and emergency flotation devices for all passengers. Our skippers hold RYA Yachtmaster certification or equivalent.

Weather monitoring happens daily via Marine Weather Services broadcasts. If conditions exceed safe operating parameters, the itinerary adjusts — the Andaman coast has enough sheltered anchorages that a route change rarely disrupts the overall program.

In-water safety protocols require a minimum of two adults on watch whenever students are swimming or snorkeling. No solo entries are permitted at any time.

## Environmental responsibility

The Andaman's coral reefs are under pressure from warming seas, anchoring damage, and tourism volume. Our program is built around low-impact practices: mooring buoys instead of anchoring on reefs, rigid waste management protocols, and a no-touch policy for coral and marine life.

Students leave with an understanding of the pressures facing Andaman marine ecosystems and the management strategies being deployed to address them.
