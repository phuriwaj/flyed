## Why flyed runs Chiang Mai trips

Our Chiang Mai program started in 2016 when our co-founder, a former IB MYP teacher, partnered with a local NGO to place student groups in rural schools outside the old city. We now work with three partner schools and two environmental organizations. Our local coordinator grew up in the city and has led over 200 school groups through Chiang Mai and Chiang Rai.

## What students do

- English-teaching placement at a partner primary school in the Doi Saket district
- River-clean fieldwork on the Ping River with environmental science curriculum notes
- Night Bazaar walking session and Thai language scavenger hunt
- Doi Suthep temple visit with a local historian guide
- Homestay overnight with a rural family outside the old city

## Logistics

- Best months: November–February (cool, dry, hazy-free)
- Burning season: March–May — we monitor haze daily and adjust outdoor activities
- Monsoon: June–October — rivers are high, jungle walks are lush
- Visa: 30-day exemption for most nationalities; extendable to 60 days
- Travel: direct flights from Bangkok run 1 hour 10 minutes; overnight train available
