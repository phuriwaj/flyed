## Why flyed runs Sukhothai trips

We added Sukhothai to our program in 2020 after curriculum feedback from IB and IGCSE teachers who cited the Sukhothai period as a required history topic. Our Sukhothai guide is a historian specializing in 13th–14th century Thai art and architecture. We work with the Ramkhamhaeng Institute, which houses a museum and research center dedicated to the Sukhothai period's development of the Thai script.

## What students do

- Sukhothai Historical Park bicycle tour with an archaeologist guide
- Wat Mahathat and Wat Si Chum visits — iconic Buddha images and lotus pond
- Ramkhamhaeng Inscription stone visit with a local historian
- Ramkhamhaeng Institute workshop: students practice writing Thai script as it was developed in the Sukhothai period
- Old city canal boat ride with views of temple ruins from the water

## Logistics

- Best months: November–February (cool, dry — comfortable for cycling and outdoor touring)
- Hot season: March–May — midday heat makes outdoor touring difficult; we schedule dawn visits
- Monsoon: June–October — lotus ponds are full and green, but some outdoor sites can be muddy
- Visa: standard 30-day exemption
- Travel: 4-hour drive or 6-hour train from Bangkok; we often fly to Phitsanulok and drive 1 hour
