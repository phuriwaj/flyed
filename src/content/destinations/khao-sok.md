## Why flyed runs Khao Sok trips

Khao Sok is our most immersive nature destination. We partnered with the national park's community-based tourism program in 2020 to build a curriculum-aligned ecology track. Our local guide is a forest ranger with a biology background who leads the rainforest night walk and can identify most of the park's bird and mammal species. All lake activities are run by a local boat-operator cooperative.

## What students do

- Cheow Lan Lake kayak and swim — water quality testing using professional sensors
- Rainforest night walk with biodiversity recording (frogs, insects, mammals)
- Limestone karst geology brief — students examine fossil deposits in the limestone
- Water-vine drawing in the forest — learn how local communities use forest plants
- Bird-watching session at dawn — Khao Sok has 180+ recorded bird species

## Logistics

- Best months: November–April (dry, lake levels high)
- Rainy season: May–October — heavy rain can raise lake levels suddenly; some trails close
- Physical difficulty: moderate — jungle trails are uneven, heat and humidity are significant
- Visa: standard 30-day exemption
- Travel: 3-hour drive from Phuket or Krabi; longer in rainy season due to road conditions
