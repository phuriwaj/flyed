## Why flyed runs Isan trips

We launched our Isan program in 2021, initially as a cultural immersion extension from Bangkok before developing a dedicated service-learning track. Our local coordinator in Buriram is a former teacher who has worked with rural schools in the region for over a decade. We partner with schools in the Surin province for English-teaching placements and with a community organization in Ubon Ratchathani for Mekong River environmental work.

## What students do

- Khmer temple visit at Phimai Historical Park with an archaeology guide
- English-teaching placement at a partner school in Surin province
- Mekong River community visit in Ubon Ratchathani — includes a local fishing community lunch
- Silk-weaving cooperative tour in Buriram — students learn about Isan's famous silk-production tradition
- Srivichai Khmer Buddha image workshop with a local artisan

## Logistics

- Best months: November–February (cool, dry — most comfortable for outdoor touring)
- Hot season: March–May — Isan is hotter than the north or south at this time; temperatures can reach 40C
- Monsoon: June–October — heavy rain but less disruptive than in the south; some rural roads become impassable
- Visa: standard 30-day exemption
- Travel: domestic flight to Buriram Airport (1 hour from Bangkok); Ubon Ratchathani reachable by flight or overnight train
