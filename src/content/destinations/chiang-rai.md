## Why flyed runs Chiang Rai trips

We've been running Chiang Rai programs since 2018, initially as an extension of our Chiang Mai service weeks. We partnered with the Mae Sai community development office and a local NGO working on sustainable agriculture with Akha and Yao communities. Our Chiang Rai guide is a local historian specializing in the Golden Triangle's 20th-century history.

## What students do

- English tutoring session at a partner school in Mae Sai district
- Sustainable agriculture workshop with a hill-tribe cooperative — planting, harvesting, or processing depending on season
- White Temple (Wat Rong Khun) architectural visit with art-history context
- Golden Triangle viewpoint and Opium Museum visit
- Border-market visit to Tachileik (Myanmar) with cross-cultural debrief

## Logistics

- Best months: November–February (cool, dry)
- Burning season: March–May — we limit outdoor activities during peak haze
- Monsoon: June–October — lush but muddy; some rural roads become difficult
- Visa: same 30-day exemption as Chiang Mai; border crossing requires special arrangement
- Travel: 3-hour drive from Chiang Mai; domestic flight to Chiang Rai airport available
