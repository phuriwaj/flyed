## Why flyed runs Pai trips

We started running Pai programs in 2018 as an alternative to the more structured Chiang Mai itinerary. Pai's small scale means we can run more flexible, adventure-oriented days. Our local guide is a Pai-born trekking guide who knows the canyon and river routes intimately. We work with a family-run riverside camp for overnight camping and river activities.

## What students do

- Canyon trek at Pai Canyon with geology context on sandstone formation
- Lod Cave exploration — guided walk through a cave system with stalactite formations and bat colonies
- River tubing and white-waterrafting on the Pai River (Class I–II rapids)
- Huai Nam Dang viewpoint sunrise trip
- Overnight camping at a riverside camp with stargazing and campfire session

## Logistics

- Best months: November–February (cool, dry — best for trekking and outdoor activities)
- Hot season: March–May — Pai stays cooler due to altitude, but still warm
- Monsoon: June–October — trails are muddy; river levels are higher for rafting
- Road note: the 762-curve mountain road from Chiang Mai causes motion sickness in some students; we schedule rest stops
- Visa: standard 30-day exemption
- Travel: 3-hour minibus from Chiang Mai; domestic flight to Mae Hong Son then 1-hour drive for some groups
