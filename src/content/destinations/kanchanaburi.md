## Why flyed runs Kanchanaburi trips

We started running Kanchanaburi history programs in 2014. Our local historian-guide is a retired Thai history teacher who has spent 20 years researching the Death Railway and the surrounding WWII sites. We work with the JEATH War Museum and a local community that runs heritage walks along the River Kwai. Our programs are designed to give students a serious historical experience — not a tourist tour.

## What students do

- Death Railway station and Hellfire Pass memorial visit with a historian-guided walk
- River Kwai bridge and Erawan Shrine visit
- JEATH War Museum tour led by local guides with family connections to the period
- Hellfire Pass interpretation centre — students work through primary-source documents
- River Kwai boat trip with local boatmen — includes a visit to a nearby Mon village

## Logistics

- Best months: November–February (cool, dry — most comfortable for outdoor activities)
- Hot season: March–May — temperatures can exceed 35C; early-morning touring recommended
- Monsoon: June–October — River Kwai runs high and fast; some sites less accessible
- Visa: standard 30-day exemption
- Travel: 2.5-hour minibus from Bangkok; train from Bangkok's Thonburi station runs 3 hours
