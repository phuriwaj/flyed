## Why flyed runs Ayutthaya trips

Our Ayutthaya program launched in 2013, built around the city's UNESCO status and its value as an open-air history classroom. Our local guide is an archaeologist who has worked on excavation sites in the old city. We use the Chao Phraya river boat as our primary transport within the island city — it's faster than road and gives students a sense of how Ayutthaya was organized around waterways.

## What students do

- Wat Mahathat and Wat Ratchaburana visit with an archaeologist guide
- Chao Phraya river boat circuit around the island city with a hydrology brief
- Ayutthaya Historical Park bicycle tour with stops at lesser-visited temple sites
- Bang Pa-In Summer Palace visit
- Local artisan workshop: traditional Thai pottery or weaving with a community cooperative

## Logistics

- Best months: November–February (cool, dry — comfortable for cycling and walking tours)
- Hot season: March–May — midday heat can be intense; we schedule outdoor activities for mornings
- Monsoon: May–October — occasional flooding on low-lying temple sites; we monitor conditions daily
- Visa: standard 30-day exemption
- Travel: 1.5-hour drive or train from Bangkok; river boat from Bangkok's Khlong Saeng Saek pier
